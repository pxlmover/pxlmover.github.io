Reactive Materials - Emission
Emission is the most popular material attributes to make AudioReactive, and for good reason.
It's really really cool.
Any material instance that has the emissive function in the master material, will get these rollout attributes with fine control over the emissive reaction.
I tried to make the parameters as self-explanatory as possible. In this example I raise the "Offset" to 1 which increases the brightness that allows me to see my mask and texture tiling without simulating or playing my level.
Once I'm done, I'll set the Offset back to 0. It sets a 'floor/base' brightness on your emission, if it helps to think of it that way.
The Threshold is handy for preventing reaction under a certain value, allowing quieter moments to pass without triggering a reaction.
Reactive Materials - Instance Parameters
There are several other reactive functions for Base Color, Roughness, Displacement, and WPO. These functions are fairly simple and multiply incoming audio by your textures and that feeds into the material channel.
Reactive Base Color will appear black in level until it receives audio input. The louder the audio, the more your texture will appear.
Reactive Roughness will make your material more reflective or more matte.
Reactive Displacement will push and pull your geometry based on audio input.
Displacement console commands and nanite required for reactive displacement to work.
Reactive WPO (World Position Offset) will push/pull geometry based on local object normal. (sphere exploding effect, anything exploding really)
Reactive Materials - The Audio Stuff
The function MF_AudioReactive_AudioMaster handles all the incoming float values from the Blueprint and send them to your material channels.
This material function contains 9 default Collection Parameters, each receiving float values from their respective Audio Track Index.
